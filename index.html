Overview
Abstract
Audio deepfakes pose a growing threat to speaker verification systems and media integrity. While existing detection systems achieve strong performance, they remain opaque black boxes offering no insight into why a sample is flagged as spoofed. In this work, we present an explainable framework for audio deepfake detection that identifies and categorises specific acoustic artefacts left behind by generative models. Our approach maps detections onto an interpretable artefact taxonomy spanning articulation, linguistic/paralinguistic, temporal/prosodic, signal-level, and spectral/voice-quality dimensions, providing human-understandable rationale for each prediction.

Slurred Speech ART-002
Prompt

"She walked to the store and came back home."

Bonafide
Spoof
Word boundaries are merged and blended. The synthesiser fails to produce distinct inter-word silence intervals, causing adjacent phonemes to coalesce in a way uncharacteristic of natural speech.
Mispronounced Words / Acronyms LNG-001
Prompt

"The distance is 5 km from here."

Bonafide
Spoof
"km" is pronounced as literal letters "KM" instead of "kilometers". The model detects a mismatch between the expected prosodic pattern for a unit abbreviation and the produced phoneme sequence.
Word / Phrase Repetition LNG-002
Prompt

"I am going to the party."

Bonafide
Spoof
Unintentional repetition: the output produces "I am going am going to the party". The model identifies repeated token sequences in the output alignment absent from the reference transcript.
Unnatural Paralinguistic Sounds LNG-003
Prompt

"That is a really great idea."

Bonafide
Spoof
Unnatural filler sounds like "huh" or "hmm" are inserted at inappropriate positions. The model detects non-lexical vocalisations with spectral profiles inconsistent with the surrounding phonetic context.
Awkward Pauses / Word Stress TMP-001
Prompt

"Hey there! I want to say goodbye."

Bonafide
Spoof
A pause occurs mid-phrase, disrupting natural flow: "Hey there I want to say... goodbye" instead of the expected grouping. The model detects silence at a syntactically implausible position inconsistent with natural speech rhythm.
Missing Natural Pauses / Breath Timing TMP-002
Prompt

"She walked in, looked around, and smiled at everyone."

Bonafide
Spoof
Overly fluent with no subtle breath moments or micro-pauses at clause boundaries. Natural speech shows consistent breathing patterns absent here, making the output sound rushed and robotic.
Lack of Expressiveness / Flat Intonation TMP-003
Prompt

"I cannot believe this is happening right now!"

Bonafide
Spoof
Monotonous speaking style with no expressive variation. The F0 contour is nearly flat throughout, lacking the pitch rises expected in exclamatory utterances. Near-zero F0 variance is flagged as a strong spoof indicator.
Background Noise / Quantization Noise SIG-001
Prompt

"The weather today is sunny and warm."

Bonafide
Spoof
Continuous background hiss/buzz interferes with speech clarity. The model detects stationary noise floor elevation in frequency bands above 6 kHz, a typical artefact of vocoder quantization.
Impulse Noise (Click / Pop / Thud / Buzz) SIG-002
Prompt

"Please turn off the lights when you leave."

Bonafide
Spoof
Short, sudden burst-like clicks appear at irregular intervals. The model identifies transient energy spikes inconsistent with phoneme boundaries, a hallmark of autoregressive neural codec decoding errors.
Muffled Voice SPC-001
Prompt

"Can you hear me clearly from there?"

Bonafide
Spoof
Voice sounds suppressed, giving the impression of a covered or distant speaker. Significant high-frequency attenuation above 4 kHz detected, consistent with low-pass filtering introduced by certain neural vocoders.
Unnatural Voice Timbre SPC-002
Prompt

"The stars are bright tonight."

Bonafide
Spoof
Voice texture is mechanical and overly sharp. Harmonic-to-noise ratio is unnaturally high, and the spectral envelope shows regularised harmonics inconsistent with human vocal fold vibration.
Distorted / Wobbly Voice SPC-003
Prompt

"Everything is going to be just fine."

Bonafide
Spoof
Voice is stretched, distorted, or shaky/unstable. Periodic F0 modulation inconsistent with natural vibrato detected, a characteristic wobble introduced by phase reconstruction errors in GAN-based vocoders.
