Overview
Abstract
Audio deepfakes — synthetically generated or voice-converted speech — pose a growing threat to speaker verification systems and media integrity. While existing detection systems achieve strong performance, they remain opaque black boxes offering no insight into why a sample is flagged as spoofed. In this work, we present an explainable framework for audio deepfake detection that identifies and categorises specific acoustic artefacts left behind by generative models. Our approach maps detections onto an interpretable artefact taxonomy spanning spectral, temporal, prosodic, formant, and glottal dimensions, providing human-understandable rationale for each prediction. Experiments on [dataset] demonstrate competitive detection performance alongside faithful and interpretable explanations.

Spectral S-001

"The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog."

Bonafide
Spoof
Unnatural spectral flatness detected in the 4–8 kHz band. The vocoder produces overly smooth high-frequency harmonics inconsistent with natural glottal excitation. Confidence: 94.2%
Spectral S-002

"She sells sea shells by the sea shore."

Bonafide
Spoof
Periodic spectral artefact at ~6.4 kHz suggests neural vocoder phase discontinuities. The sibilant /s/ shows reduced stochastic variability typical of TTS oversmoothing. Confidence: 91.7%
Spectral S-003

"How much wood would a woodchuck chuck?"

Bonafide
Spoof
Mel-spectrogram shows grid-like aliasing pattern at frame boundaries — a known artefact of WaveNet-style autoregressive decoders. Confidence: 88.9%
Temporal T-001

"Please call Stella, ask her to bring these things."

Bonafide
Spoof
Unnatural inter-phoneme pause durations detected at word boundaries. The rhythm model produces overly uniform inter-word gaps that deviate from natural speech rhythm distributions. Confidence: 89.5%
Temporal T-002

"North winds prevail in January."

Bonafide
Spoof
Micro-temporal jitter absent in the spoofed sample. Natural speech exhibits ~2–4 ms variability in voiced segment durations; synthesised sample shows near-zero variance. Confidence: 86.1%
Prosodic P-001

"I can't believe you actually said that to her!"

Bonafide
Spoof
Monotonic F0 contour with insufficient emphasis on the focus word "actually". Natural exclamatory utterances show sharp F0 peaks (ΔF0 > 40 Hz) absent in the synthesised version. Confidence: 92.3%
Formant F-001

"Are you going to the market this evening?"

Bonafide
Spoof
F2–F3 formant transitions are unnaturally abrupt during vowel-to-consonant coarticulation. The acoustic model does not reproduce the continuous formant movement characteristic of natural vocal tract dynamics. Confidence: 87.4%
Glottal G-001

"The sun rises in the east every morning."

Bonafide
Spoof
Missing glottal pulse irregularity (jitter & shimmer). Synthesised glottal waveform is overly periodic; human voices exhibit natural cycle-to-cycle variation (jitter ~0.5–1%) absent here. Confidence: 90.8%
